Kevin Indig shared insights from an independent UX study analyzing how users interact with Google's AI Overviews.

Key takeaways:

- Around 80% of users still reach their final answer through organic search results.
- Most users only view the top portion of AI Overviews.
- Brand trust has become more important than simple keyword relevance.
- Younger users are more likely to engage with AI Overviews than older users.
- SEO teams should measure visibility inside AI Overviews instead of focusing only on rankings.
- Building authority across platforms like Reddit and YouTube is becoming increasingly important.

## Post 2 — Zero-click searches & the decline of search traffic

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_zero-click-searches-have-jumped-from-72-activity-7325214522384728065-DZo2

**Theme:** Traffic is falling, so SEO success needs new metrics.

### Summary

Kevin shared data showing that zero-click searches continue to grow, meaning users are getting answers directly on Google without visiting websites. He explains that while overall traffic may decrease, the visitors who do click are likely to have stronger intent. Instead of focusing only on traffic, businesses should start measuring brand visibility, AI citations, and overall influence across search platforms.

### Why it matters

This post shows how AI is changing the way SEO performance should be measured. Traditional traffic reports are becoming less useful, while visibility in AI-powered search experiences is becoming more important.

## Post 3 — AI Mode study: visibility matters more than clicks

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_all-of-my-clients-have-asked-me-about-my-activity-7331421919147114496-qH3V

**Theme:** Insights from 250 AI Mode search sessions.

### Summary

Kevin shared findings from his AI Mode research, where he observed how people interact with Google's AI-powered search experience. He found that informational searches generated very few clicks to external websites because users often received complete answers directly within AI Mode. He suggests that marketers should start tracking brand influence, pipeline impact, and AI visibility instead of relying only on rankings and website sessions.

### Why it matters

The study highlights that success in AI search is becoming less about driving clicks and more about making sure your brand appears as a trusted source.

## Post 4 — SEO is shifting from rankings to brand authority

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_for-over-a-decade-the-seo-equation-was-linear-activity-7419363929501614080-LDye

**Theme:** The traditional SEO model is changing.

### Summary

Kevin explains that the old SEO approach of increasing rankings to generate more traffic is becoming less reliable because AI can answer many questions without sending users to websites. ### Why it matters

This reinforces the idea that future SEO strategies need to focus on building trust and visibility across different platforms rather than depending only on organic search traffic.

## Additional Insight — AI Content Production

**Source:** https://www.searchenginejournal.com/the-science-of-how-ai-pays-attention/567597/

### Summary

Kevin shared research from a joint AirOps study that analyzed more than 815,000 prompt-page pairs to understand what type of content AI assistants prefer to cite. The research found that focused articles answering specific questions were cited more often than long "ultimate guide" style content. It also suggested that concise, well-structured articles between 500 and 2,000 words performed better for AI citations than extremely long articles.

### Why it matters

This research provides practical guidance for creating AI-friendly content. Instead of producing longer articles, marketers should focus on writing clear, focused content that directly answers user questions.
