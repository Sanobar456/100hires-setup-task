# Cyrus Shepard — LinkedIn Research Notes

## Post 1 — AI Citation Ranking Factors (May 2026)

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/cyrusshepard/

**Supporting Article:** https://signal.zyppy.com/p/ai-citation-ranking-factors

Cyrus published one of the most detailed studies on AI citation ranking factors by reviewing more than 50 experiments, research papers, patents, and industry case studies.

Instead of relying on opinions, he ranked each factor based on available evidence.

Some of the strongest factors included:

- Making pages easily accessible for AI crawlers.
- Ranking well in traditional search results.
- Appearing in Google's fan-out searches.
- Using proper preview controls.
- Writing content that directly answers user questions.

One interesting finding was that LLMs.txt had almost no proven impact despite the amount of discussion around it.

His overall conclusion is that traditional SEO still matters. Strong SEO creates the foundation, while AI visibility requires additional optimization around relevance, trust, authority, and extractable content.

## Post 2 — Why Click Signals Still Matter (March 2026)

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cyrusshepard_is-your-content-effort-a-google-ranking-activity-7211068066737061888-d6-m

**Supporting Article:** https://signal.zyppy.com/p/google-click-signals

Cyrus shared research showing that user behavior continues to influence both Google rankings and AI-generated answers.

Based on Google's antitrust documents and supporting experiments, he explains that Google measures user satisfaction through signals like the final click before someone stops searching.

Rather than focusing only on keywords, websites should improve user experience by matching search intent quickly, loading fast, and avoiding intrusive elements.

His key message is that improving user satisfaction also increases the likelihood of appearing inside AI-generated responses.

## Post 3 — What Separates High-Quality Pages (2024)

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cyrusshepard_big-shakeup-for-google-quality-raters-google-activity-7155009356210622465-hCnt

Cyrus analyzed thousands of data points across multiple websites to understand which page characteristics consistently appeared on better-performing sites.

Some recurring patterns included:

- Less aggressive advertising.
- Clear navigation.
- Evidence of first-hand experience.
- Personal insights instead of generic writing.

His experience as a former Google Quality Rater gives additional context to these findings because he understands how quality signals have historically been evaluated.

The biggest takeaway is that helpful, experience-based content continues to outperform generic content, even as search evolves with AI.
