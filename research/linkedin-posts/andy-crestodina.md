## Post 1 — How the Best B2B Companies Build Their Content Strategy

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/andycrestodina_how-the-top-1-of-b2b-companies-do-content-activity-7166463707177213952-jQ_t

**Theme:** Building a content strategy that consistently produces better results

### Key Insights

Andy explains that the highest-performing B2B companies don't rely on SEO alone. Instead, they combine several activities into one strategy, including search optimization, original research, expert contributions, digital PR, content promotion, and ongoing performance analysis.

One idea that stood out to me is that publishing great content isn't enough anymore. If nobody discovers it, the quality doesn't matter. Promotion has become just as important as creation.

He also emphasizes creating original research because unique statistics naturally attract backlinks, media mentions, and AI citations. Instead of repeating existing information, brands should become the source others reference.

Another practical recommendation is adding expert quotes to articles. Besides improving credibility and trust, contributors often share articles they're featured in, giving the content additional reach.

Andy also points out that putting in extra effort consistently separates high-performing content from average content.

## Post 2 — How Content Marketers Are Actually Using AI

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/andycrestodina_whats-most-effective-content-strategy-2025-activity-qtjkc

**Supporting article:** https://www.orbitmedia.com/blog/ai-uses-for-content-marketing/

**Theme:** AI is becoming an editing and research assistant rather than a replacement for writers.

### Key Insights

Orbit Media's annual survey shows AI adoption among content marketers has grown rapidly, with almost every marketer now using AI somewhere in their workflow.

What surprised me most is that editing has become the most common AI use case, overtaking idea generation. Many marketers now use AI to improve clarity, grammar, and structure rather than asking it to write complete articles.

Only a small percentage rely on AI to generate entire blog posts. Most use it for brainstorming, keyword research, SEO improvements, content audits, and editing.

Andy also notes that there is still no strong evidence that simply using AI leads to better-performing content. Strategy, expertise, and originality continue to have a much bigger impact than automation.

His perspective is that AI works best as a collaborator. It can help organize ideas and speed up parts of the process, but it cannot replace human opinions, experience, or unique insights.

## Post 3 — The Real SEO Problem Isn't Rankings, It's Click-Through Rate

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/andycrestodina/

**Supporting article:** https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/whats-most-effective-content-strategy-2025-new-report-andy-crestodina-qtjkc

**Theme:** Organic visibility is changing because fewer people click search results.

### Key Insights

Andy has collected Google search result screenshots over many years, showing how ads, AI Overviews, local packs, shopping results, and other SERP features continue pushing organic listings lower on the page.

His conclusion is that the biggest SEO challenge today isn't achieving higher rankings—it's getting users to click at all.

Rather than fighting these platform changes, Orbit Media expanded its publishing efforts through a LinkedIn Newsletter, allowing them to reach a much larger audience directly on the platform.

Although this approach reduced website traffic, it significantly increased overall visibility and audience reach.

I found this interesting because it supports the same direction discussed by Amanda Natividad and Wil Reynolds: success is becoming less about maximizing website visits and more about building awareness, trust, and visibility wherever the audience spends time.
