## Post 1 — Content Automation Supports Marketers, Not Replaces Them

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_dear-social-media-managers-please-use-activity-7473049322587230208-WECi

**Date:** June 2026

**Theme:** AI should automate repetitive tasks while humans focus on creativity and strategy.

### Summary

Ross explains that AI is most valuable when it removes repetitive work such as drafting, formatting, and content repurposing. He introduces Distribution.ai as a tool designed to automate these repetitive tasks while allowing marketers to spend more time on strategy, creative thinking, and original ideas.

### Why it matters

This reinforces the idea that AI should improve productivity rather than replace human creativity, making content teams more efficient without reducing quality.

## Post 2 — Research, Create, Then Distribute

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_research-your-audience-and-industry-create-activity-7472622763208511489-hLdY

**Date:** June 2026

**Theme:** Effective content marketing requires continuous distribution, not just content creation.

### Summary

Ross shares a simple content framework: research your audience, create valuable content, and continuously distribute it across multiple channels. He emphasizes using customer conversations, reviews, online communities, and industry discussions to generate ideas before creating content. He also explains that successful distribution now includes visibility across AI assistants as well as traditional marketing channels.

### Why it matters

The framework highlights that content success depends on both quality and consistent distribution, especially as AI-powered search becomes more common.

## Post 3 — Why Unscalable Content Wins

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_why-creating-unscalable-content-amidst-the-activity-7155173252296790016-49hP

**Date:** January 2024

**Theme:** Original content becomes more valuable as AI-generated content increases.

### Summary

Ross argues that businesses should invest in content that AI cannot easily replicate, including original research, customer stories, first-hand experience, interviews, and unique perspectives. As AI makes generic content easier to produce, authentic content becomes a stronger competitive advantage.

### Why it matters

This post explains why businesses should focus on creating unique, experience-based content that builds trust and stands out from large volumes of AI-generated material.
