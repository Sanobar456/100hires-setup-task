## Post 1 — Zero-Click Searches Hit 68% in Early 2026

**Date:** Q4 2025 / Early 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_the-q4-2025-state-of-search-from-datos-a-activity-7422003466606239744-UsJ7

**Theme:** Zero-click searches continue to grow as AI changes search behavior.

### Summary

Rand Fishkin shared findings from SparkToro and Similarweb showing that 68.01% of Google searches in the U.S. ended without a click during the first months of 2026. The study also found that AI Overviews now appear on more than 20% of Google searches and significantly reduce click-through rates. Rand argues that AI is not replacing Google Search, but it is changing how users interact with search results.

### Key Takeaways

* 68.01% of Google searches ended without a click.
* Zero-click searches increased significantly compared to 2024.
* AI Overviews reduce click-through rates by around 60%.
* Only a small portion of searches now send visitors to independent websites.
* AI search is changing traffic patterns rather than replacing traditional search.

**Supporting Resource**

https://datos.live/report/state-of-search-q3-2025/

### Why it matters

This research highlights why marketers should focus less on traffic alone and more on overall visibility, brand awareness, and presence within AI-powered search experiences.

## Post 2 — Does a Zero-Click Web Mean There's No Point in Content?

**Date:** 2025

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_does-a-zero-click-web-mean-theres-activity-7330472234509111297-E9d9

**Theme:** Brand-building content matters more than traffic-focused content.

### Summary

Rand explains that zero-click search does not eliminate the value of content. Instead, it changes the purpose of content marketing. Rather than creating content solely to generate website visits, businesses should create content that builds trust, familiarity, and brand recognition across the platforms where their audience already spends time.

### Key Takeaways

* Zero-click search rewards strong brands rather than traffic-focused strategies.
* Businesses should understand where their audience spends time before creating content.
* Content should influence buyers before they perform branded searches.
* Visibility across communities, podcasts, social platforms, and AI tools is becoming increasingly valuable.

### Why it matters

This reinforces the shift from measuring SEO success through website traffic to measuring overall brand influence across multiple digital channels.

## Post 3 — The Downstream Impact of AI Visibility

**Date:** 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_the-death-of-clicks-rand-fishkin-on-brand-activity-7468575767635890176-znz0

**Theme:** AI visibility creates brand awareness that later drives conversions.

### Summary

Rand shared research showing that brands mentioned in AI-generated answers often experience increased branded searches and higher conversions, even when users never click directly from AI platforms. He argues that traditional attribution models fail to capture this indirect influence because customers frequently discover brands through AI before returning later through branded searches.

### Key Takeaways

* AI mentions increase brand awareness.
* Increased brand awareness leads to more branded searches.
* Traditional attribution models often miss this customer journey.
* New measurement approaches should include AI mentions, share of voice, audience growth, and post-purchase discovery surveys.

**Supporting Resource**

Similarweb × SparkToro Downstream AI Visibility Report (2026)

### Why it matters

This research demonstrates that AI visibility can create measurable business value even when it does not generate immediate website clicks, reinforcing the importance of long-term brand building.
