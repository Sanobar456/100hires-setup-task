## Post 1 — Organic Traffic Declined, But Revenue Increased

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/wilreynolds/ (Pinned post)
**Supporting source:** https://seoweek.org/wil-reynolds/

**Theme:** Why business outcomes matter more than traffic in the AI search era

### Key Insights

Wil shared that Seer Interactive experienced a significant decline in organic traffic over two years while revenue and pipeline continued to grow.

His message is that traffic should no longer be treated as the primary measure of SEO success.

Social channels, especially LinkedIn, generated much higher-quality traffic and stronger conversion rates than organic search.

He argues that marketers should focus on business metrics such as revenue, qualified leads, and pipeline rather than search traffic alone.

One of his main observations is that search behavior is shifting from clicking websites to receiving direct answers from AI systems.

## Post 2 — UX Research on Real ChatGPT Users

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/wilreynolds/ (Activity feed)
**Supporting source:** https://www.seerinteractive.com/events

**Theme:** How people actually use AI search

### Key Insights

Seer's UX team has conducted multiple studies observing people using ChatGPT to solve real tasks.

Users are providing much more detailed prompts than they did when ChatGPT first launched.

Many prompts now include specific company or brand names, showing that brand awareness increasingly influences AI search behaviour.

AI follow-up suggestions encourage users to continue conversations instead of starting new searches.

Wil believes brands should optimize for trust and comparison queries instead of only targeting generic keywords.

## Post 3 — Build Trust Instead of Chasing AI Visibility

**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/wilreynolds/ (SEO Week 2026 posts)
**Supporting source:** https://www.seerinteractive.com/events/on-demand-wil-reynolds-at-seo-week-2026

**Theme:** Long-term trust beats short-term AI optimization tactics

### Key Insights

Wil warns against focusing only on AI visibility metrics without measuring business impact.

He believes AI-generated content without expertise or original perspective creates "zombie content" that damages brand credibility.

Instead of using AI to mass-produce articles, he recommends using it to better understand customers and improve content quality.

Brands should regularly evaluate how AI systems describe them, especially in competitor comparison queries.

Success should be measured through trust signals such as referrals, direct traffic, community discussions, and revenue—not visibility scores alone.
