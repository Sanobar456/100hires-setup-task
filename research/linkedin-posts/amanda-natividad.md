## Post 1 — Zero-Click Marketing Does Not Mean Zero Sales

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_zero-click-marketing-does-not-mean-zero-sales-activity-7437886375653253121-uwys

### Summary

Amanda explains that zero-click marketing is not about avoiding sales or website traffic. Instead, it focuses on educating and building trust directly on platforms like LinkedIn so that potential customers already know your brand before they need your product.

Some of her main points include:

* Buyers often make purchasing decisions long before they visit a company's website.
* Content should provide value first instead of trying to force every reader to click a link.
* The people who eventually visit your website are usually more qualified because they already trust your brand.
* Amanda grew her own audience to more than 100,000 followers primarily through consistent zero-click content.

### Why it matters

This post highlights how AI search and changing user behavior are making brand awareness more valuable than simply driving website traffic.

## Post 2 — Understanding the Zero-Click Marketing Framework

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_zero-click-marketing-its-a-term-youve-activity-7285806355725135873-jCM3

### Summary

Amanda defines Zero-Click Marketing as creating content that delivers value without expecting users to leave the platform. She explains that success comes from helping people understand, remember, and trust your brand before they are ready to buy.

She introduces a simple customer journey:

* Seen
* Understood
* Remembered
* Preferred
* Sought out

She also explains that AI search is making this approach even more important because AI assistants often answer questions without sending users to company websites.

### Why it matters

This framework provides a practical way to think about content marketing in an AI-first world, where visibility and brand recognition often matter more than clicks.

## Post 3 — Measuring Zero-Click Marketing

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_but-if-i-do-zero-click-marketing-how-will-activity-7216456099938410496-h7dO

### Summary

Amanda discusses how marketers should measure the impact of zero-click content. Instead of focusing only on website traffic, she recommends tracking both early engagement signals and long-term business outcomes.

Examples of leading indicators include:

* Impressions
* Engagement
* Follower growth
* Branded searches
* Community mentions

Examples of long-term indicators include:

* Sales opportunities
* Pipeline influence
* Closed deals
* Customers mentioning they had been following the company's content

She also recommends asking new customers how they first heard about the company because traditional attribution often misses the impact of educational content.

### Why it matters

This post shows that measuring brand influence requires looking beyond analytics dashboards. Building awareness and trust often happens long before someone becomes a customer.
