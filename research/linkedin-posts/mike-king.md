## Post 1 — The Open Web Is Dying. Not Slowing.

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/michaelkingphilly/

**Supporting Article:** https://ipullrank.com/machine-media

### Summary

Mike King explains that the web is shifting toward what he calls **"Machine Media,"** where AI agents consume far more web content than humans. He shares data showing that AI crawlers now visit thousands of pages for every referral they send back, while many publishers continue losing search traffic. His main argument is that businesses need to make their content accessible to AI systems because future visibility will depend on whether AI agents can retrieve and recommend their information, not just whether they rank in traditional search.

### Why it matters

This introduces a new way of thinking about SEO. Instead of optimizing only for human visitors, brands also need to optimize for AI systems that increasingly decide what information users see.

## Post 2 — How AI Mode Works and How SEO Can Prepare

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/michaelkingphilly_how-ai-mode-works-and-how-seo-can-prepare-activity-7333250164985434112-KJyj

**Supporting Article:** https://ipullrank.com/how-ai-mode-works

### Summary

Mike explains how Google's AI Mode works using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Rather than answering entirely from memory, AI retrieves relevant passages from multiple sources, converts them into vector embeddings, and generates a response based on the most relevant information. Because of this, content needs to be easy for AI systems to retrieve and understand, not just optimized for traditional keyword rankings. He argues that SEO is evolving into what he calls **"Relevance Engineering."**

### Why it matters

This helps explain why AI search requires different optimization strategies than traditional SEO. Understanding how AI retrieves and uses content can help marketers create content that is more likely to be surfaced in AI-generated answers.

## Post 3 — Blocking Your Site from Generative AI Tools Is a Mistake

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/michaelkingphilly/

### Summary

Mike argues that blocking AI crawlers such as GPTBot or ClaudeBot can reduce a brand's visibility in AI-powered search experiences. He recommends allowing trusted AI crawlers, improving JavaScript rendering, using structured data, and optimizing content at the passage level so AI systems can retrieve and understand them more effectively. He also emphasizes that website speed and technical performance are becoming increasingly important for AI visibility.

### Why it matters

This highlights that technical SEO now extends beyond Googlebot. As AI assistants become another way people discover information, ensuring AI crawlers can access and interpret content becomes an important part of SEO strategy.
