## Interview 1 — New Strategies for Improving Blog Performance

**Source:** https://www.convinceandconvert.com/content-marketing/new-strategies-for-improving-blog-performance-plus-qa-with-andy-crestodina/

**Theme:** What Orbit Media's annual blogging research reveals about AI and content performance.

### Key Insights

Andy explains that although AI adoption has increased dramatically among content marketers, it hasn't automatically led to better-performing content. Success still depends on having a clear strategy, fact-checking, and adding original thinking that AI cannot provide.

One pattern that appears consistently in Orbit Media's annual survey is that higher effort usually leads to better results. Longer articles, expert contributions, visuals, editing, and stronger promotion all tend to outperform content created with minimal effort.

Another interesting finding is that the average time spent writing a blog post has barely changed, even with widespread AI adoption. AI speeds up individual tasks, but producing high-quality content still requires significant human effort.

Andy also considers expert quotes one of the highest-value additions to any article. They improve credibility while also encouraging contributors to share the content with their own audiences, increasing distribution naturally.

His biggest recommendation for B2B companies today is to launch a LinkedIn Newsletter. As organic search clicks continue to decline, publishing directly on platforms where audiences already spend time helps maintain visibility and grow relationships with potential customers.

## Webinar — All of My Best AI Methods

**Source:** Orbit Media Webinar (with Matt Heinz)
**Published:** March 2026

### Key Insights

Andy demonstrated how he uses AI throughout the content creation process without allowing it to replace human expertise.

He recommends using AI to discover original research opportunities by identifying commonly repeated claims that lack supporting evidence. Producing original data around these topics creates valuable resources that journalists, bloggers, and AI systems are more likely to reference.

His AI-assisted workflow follows five stages:

* Research new opportunities and content gaps.
* Build a structured outline.
* Write the article with humans contributing the main ideas and insights while AI assists with supporting sections.
* Collect genuine expert quotes from industry professionals.
* Use AI during editing for clarity, readability, and SEO improvements before final human review.

Andy also explained where AI falls short. It cannot develop genuine opinions, build relationships with industry experts, conduct original research, or create the trust required for thought leadership. Those remain uniquely human strengths.

Another practical recommendation is to ensure important statistics are written in plain text rather than only displayed inside charts or images. Search engines and AI systems understand text far more reliably, making written summaries of charts valuable for both SEO and AI visibility.
