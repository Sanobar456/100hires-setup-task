# Video / Podcast 1

## The Future of SEO in an AI-First World

**Source:** https://www.churn.fm/episode/the-future-of-seo-in-an-ai-first-world-with-kevin-indig

**Published:** 2025

**Format:** Podcast (Transcript Available)

### Key Takeaways

#### Peak search traffic has likely passed

Kevin explains that traditional search traffic is declining because AI tools such as ChatGPT and Google AI Mode answer many user questions before they click through to websites. He believes the remaining traffic will be smaller in volume but much higher in intent.

---

#### Evergreen content is becoming less effective

He suggests that the traditional strategy of publishing long evergreen guides is becoming less valuable. Instead, brands should focus on building influence across multiple platforms where AI systems discover and reference information.

---

#### SEO now extends beyond websites

Kevin explains that marketers are losing visibility into the early stages of the customer journey because AI assistants answer many research questions directly. As a result, SEO should expand beyond websites into platforms such as Reddit, YouTube, and other trusted communities.

---

#### Brand recognition matters more

According to Kevin, users naturally look for familiar and trustworthy brands inside AI-generated answers, especially when making important decisions such as healthcare or financial choices.

---

#### Preparing websites for AI agents

He recommends understanding how AI crawlers and LLM user agents access websites and monitoring server logs to better understand AI-driven traffic and crawling behavior.

---

## Why this source matters

This interview provides a practical overview of how AI is changing SEO strategy, user behavior, and measurement beyond traditional keyword rankings.

# Video / Podcast 2

## Moving Beyond Old SEO Models in the Age of AI

**Source:** https://www.advancedwebranking.com/blog/beyond-old-seo-models-ai-search

**Published:** January 2026

**Format:** Podcast Transcript

### Key Takeaways

* AI assistants increasingly reference platforms such as LinkedIn, Reddit, Quora, and YouTube, making off-site content more important for visibility.

* Kevin believes Google has adapted quickly to AI search despite being initially challenged by newer AI products.

* Modern SEO should focus less on individual keywords and more on entities, topics, search intent, and strong brand signals.

* SEO is expanding into areas traditionally handled by branding, digital PR, social media, and content marketing, making collaboration across channels more important than ever.

---

## Why this source matters

This discussion highlights how SEO is evolving from a channel focused on rankings into a broader discipline centered on brand visibility, authority, and AI discoverability.
