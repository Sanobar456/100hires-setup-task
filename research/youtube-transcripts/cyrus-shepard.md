# Cyrus Shepard — Podcast & Interview Research Notes

## Interview 1 — Google Click Data & AI Rankings (April 2026)

**Podcast:** Near Memo Podcast (Episode 251)

**Source:** https://www.nearmedia.co/ep-251-google-lied-about-click-data-heres-what-actually-drives-rankings-in-2026-with-cyrus-shepard/

Cyrus explains how recent Google antitrust documents confirmed that user click behavior still plays an important role in search rankings.

One concept he highlights is the "last longest click," where Google looks at the final result users choose before ending their search session. This helps Google estimate whether the content actually solved the user's problem.

He also argues that AI-generated answers are not independent from these signals. Since Google's AI systems rely on search data when generating responses, content that performs well with users is also more likely to be selected for AI citations.

His practical advice is to satisfy user intent immediately. Pages should answer questions quickly, reduce distractions like intrusive popups, and make the first section of content genuinely useful.

Cyrus also mentions that most of his original research is now published through his Substack newsletter, making it his primary platform for sharing experiments and findings.

## Interview 2 — A New Era in SEO (March 2026)

**Podcast:** Marketing Speak

**Source:** https://www.marketingspeak.com/a-new-era-in-seo-with-cyrus-shepard/

Cyrus explains that he became a Google Quality Rater because he wanted firsthand experience with how Google evaluates content quality.

That experience helped shape his research approach, where he prefers testing ideas with real data instead of relying on assumptions.

From his studies, he found that websites demonstrating genuine experience, strong usability, and helpful content consistently performed better than pages relying on traditional SEO tricks.

He also notes that excessive optimization, such as aggressive internal linking or keyword-focused content, can work against a website if it reduces the overall user experience.

One of his biggest takeaways is that brand authority and authentic expertise are becoming much more important than technical SEO shortcuts.

## Interview 3 — Surviving the Zero-Click Era (December 2025)

**Podcast:** Lawyerist Podcast

**Source:** https://legaltalknetwork.com/podcasts/lawyerist-podcast/2025/12/surviving-the-zero-click-era-what-lawyers-must-know-about-seo-and-ai-with-cyrus-shepard/

Cyrus discusses how content strategy needs to adapt as more users get answers directly from AI systems instead of clicking through to websites.

He recommends structuring articles so important information appears early, using clear headings and self-contained sections that AI systems can easily understand and reference.

Another major point is the growing importance of brand authority. Instead of focusing only on keywords, businesses should invest in building a recognizable and trustworthy brand because AI systems increasingly rely on those signals when selecting sources.

His overall message is that modern content should be created for both people and AI retrieval systems, with clarity, credibility, and real expertise at the center.
